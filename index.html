The Founder’s Manifesto

Gaurav Bansal

Connect on LinkedIn

“दिल में हो आग तो जलती रहनी चाहिए,
तेरा हो चाहे मेरा, सपना ज़िंदा रहना चाहिए!”

Startups in India aren't built in garages; they are built at kitchen tables — amid family debates and financial anxiety.

In a country like BHARAT, the “Opportunity Cost” isn't just a line on a spreadsheet — it is a weight on a founder’s soul. For the middle-class dreamer, choosing a startup over a steady paycheck is an act of war against social security.

A startup’s greatest enemy isn’t a competitor — it’s the “Initial Days Vacuum.” We talk about “funding”, but we forget about “foundation”.

Everyone has the Keeda (the itch) and the Himmat (the courage), but courage without a compass is just a slow way to get lost.

We treat early-stage startups like athletes, but we should treat them like infants. If you don’t hold the hand that is trying to build, that hand will eventually reach for a corporate cubicle just to survive.

I believe tactical, hands-on support in the first 100 days is more valuable than a seed check in the first 300. We need to stop letting “outliers” die because they didn’t have a map.

If we provide the right scaffolding to the aspiring founder, we won’t just see more startups — we will see an era of unstoppable builders.

It doesn’t matter whose hand holds the torch, as long as the darkness is defeated. I am not here to just invest; I am here to ensure the fire doesn’t go out before the sun rises.

“I lost my startup so that you don’t have to lose yours. My failure was a lesson; my mission is your survival.”
